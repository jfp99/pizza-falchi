## Pricing Models

### Hourly Rate Projects
**When to use:** Uncertain scope, ongoing maintenance, discovery phases
**Pros:** Flexible, fair for scope changes
**Cons:** Client uncertainty about total cost
**Structure:** ‚Ç¨80-120/hour + weekly time reports

### Fixed-Price Projects ‚≠ê **RECOMMENDED**
**When to use:** Clear requirements, defined deliverables
**Pros:** Client knows exact cost, you can profit from efficiency
**Cons:** Scope creep risk
**Structure:**

**Small Projects (1-3 weeks):**
- ‚Ç¨2,000-‚Ç¨6,000
- Examples: Landing pages, small business sites, simple integrations

**Medium Projects (4-8 weeks):**
- ‚Ç¨8,000-‚Ç¨20,000
- Examples: E-commerce platforms, SaaS MVPs, custom dashboards

**Large Projects (10-16 weeks):**
- ‚Ç¨25,000-‚Ç¨50,000
- Examples: Complex e-commerce, multi-tenant SaaS, enterprise apps

**Calculation Formula:**
```
Estimated Hours √ó Hourly Rate √ó 1.2 (buffer) = Fixed Price
```

Example:
- E-commerce estimate: 120 hours
- Your rate: ‚Ç¨100/hour
- Buffer: 1.2 (20% contingency)
- Fixed price: 120 √ó ‚Ç¨100 √ó 1.2 = ‚Ç¨14,400

**Payment Structure:**
- 30% upfront (milestone 1: design + planning)
- 40% at mid-point (milestone 2: core features)
- 30% on completion (milestone 3: testing + launch)

### Retainer Agreements (Month 3+)
**When to use:** Ongoing maintenance, monthly feature development
**Pros:** Predictable income, long-term relationships
**Cons:** Commitment, opportunity cost

**Bronze:** ‚Ç¨1,500/month (15-20 hours)

- Bug fixes + small features + performance monitoring
